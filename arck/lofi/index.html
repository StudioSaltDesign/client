The challenge today

Power is getting scarcer, dirtier, and more expensive.

Demand is exploding — driven by AI — while the grid strains and today's clean options fall short. Hydrogen could fill the gap, but storing and using it efficiently has always been the obstacle.

Pain 01
The grid can't keep up

Grid power runs $0.20–$0.30/kWh and climbing, outages are rising, and AI data-center demand is growing 30–35%+ a year — behind multi-year interconnection queues.

Pain 02
Clean options fall short

Batteries store a fixed charge and deplete. Conventional "hot" fuel cells waste much of their energy as heat, degrade over time, and land near $0.52/kWh.

Pain 03
Hydrogen stays locked away

Compressed and liquid hydrogen are costly, energy-hungry to store, and carry a heavy safety profile — which is why clean hydrogen has stayed "ten years away."

The breakthrough

What if hydrogen was the storage?

Hydrogen becomes its own storage, held safely in solid state, inside a fuel cell that runs cool instead of hot. The result is a continuous power source that keeps producing — not a battery that depletes.

Technology / PEM+ Membrane
Innovation 01 · The foundation

PEM+ Membrane.

The heart of every fuel cell, redesigned — moving energy with less waste and far more durability, so the whole platform can run cool and efficient.

PRODUCT HERO ILLUSTRATION
isometric cutaway of the PEM+ membrane —
microporous ionomer with internal
reinforcing pillars (cool tones, warm accent)
Why it matters

Most fuel cells wear out from the inside.

Inside every fuel cell, a thin membrane carries hydrogen's energy into electricity — but run it hard or hot and it swells and cracks. That's the number-one reason fuel cells wear out and stay too costly to be practical.

COMPARISON — a conventional membrane swelling and cracking under stress vs. the PEM+ staying stable (wide, abstract)
How it works

Charge moves through; the structure holds firm.

Three things happen at once, every moment the cell runs.

Hydrogen meets the membrane surface and splits, freeing the charged particles that carry its energy across.

The microporous ionomer passes that charge with very low resistance, wasting little as heat. That's the root of the efficiency.

Built-in reinforcing pillars keep the membrane stable under load, resisting the swelling and cracking that wear cells out.

STEP 1 — hydrogen splitting at the membrane
surface, charged particles released
STEP 2 — charge crossing the microporous
ionomer with low resistance (little heat)
STEP 3 — reinforcing pillars holding the
membrane stable under load
What makes it different

Why the membrane is the moat.

Efficiency

Less wasted energy

Lower internal resistance means more of the hydrogen becomes electricity, not heat — a key reason the system reaches up to 85% efficiency.

Durability

Built to last

A reinforced structure controls the swelling and cracking that shorten the life of conventional membranes.

Defensibility

Deep patent moat

PEM+ is protected by an extensive IP portfolio — over 2,300 pages of patents filed internationally.

Technology / Advanced Fuel Cell
Innovation 02 · The engine

Advanced Fuel Cell.

The world's first fuel cell that runs cool — spreading the work across thin micro-stacks so more hydrogen becomes electricity, not heat.

PRODUCT HERO ILLUSTRATION
isometric of the CoolEnergy™ µStack /
Advanced Fuel Cell — low-profile
stacked micro-cells (cool tones, warm accent)
Why it matters

Conventional fuel cells run too hot to be useful.

Most fuel cells stack into a tall tower that traps heat in the center, reaching 100°C to 1,000°C. That heat is wasted energy — it degrades the cell, demands heavy cooling, and is a fire risk, keeping fuel cells out of homes, offices, and cities.

COMPARISON — a tall "hot" stack with heat concentrated in the center vs. ARCK's low-profile micro-stacks spreading heat out (wide, heat-map style)
How it works

It comes down to geometry.

Spread the work out, and the heat goes away.

Instead of one tall tower, the cell uses many thin micro-stacks (µStacks), distributing the load so heat never concentrates.

Each µStack conducts heat outward, so the cell runs around 50°C — closer to warm than hot, with almost no cooling needed.

Because little energy is lost as heat, up to 85% becomes electricity — using about 30% less hydrogen than a hot cell.

STEP 1 — many thin µStacks replacing one
tall tower (load distributed evenly)
STEP 2 — heat conducting outward,
cell running cool at ~50°C
STEP 3 — up to 85% to electricity,
~30% less hydrogen used
What makes it different

Cooler is the whole point.

Efficiency

Up to 85%

About 40% more efficient than conventional PEM fuel cells — more electricity from every gram of hydrogen.

Heat

17–33× less heat

Runs near room temperature (~50°C), so it needs almost no cooling and carries no fire risk.

Deployment

Goes anywhere

Cool, quiet, clean operation makes it safe for homes, offices, and dense urban sites where hot cells can't go.

At a glance

The essentials.

What it is
CoolEnergy™ µStack — a low-temperature Advanced Fuel Cell built on the PEM+ membrane.

40–65°C, typically ~50°C (vs 100–1,000°C for hot cells).

~30% less than a hot cell for the same power.

Fire-safe, minimal cooling — no fumes or toxins.

Technology / Intelligent Buffered Fuel Cell
Innovation 03 · The intelligence

Intelligent Buffered Fuel Cell.

The world's first self-recharging battery — smart electronics let the fuel cell run steady while a built-in buffer handles every surge, keeping power stable 24/7.

PRODUCT HERO ILLUSTRATION
isometric cutaway of the iBFC —
fuel cell + battery buffer + intelligent
charge-transfer electronics (cool tones, warm accent)
Why it matters

Real power demand is bursty. Fuel cells hate that.

Real demand is bursty — an AC kicks on, a server spikes — but fuel cells can't follow sudden swings without their efficiency collapsing (from 84% to 32%) and overheating. That gap is what's kept fuel cells stuck in the lab.

DIAGRAM — direct drive: load spikes force the fuel cell's current to swing, efficiency crashing from 84% to 32% (wide, a jagged unstable line)
How it works

A battery that recharges itself.

The fuel cell never powers your devices directly — here's the trick.

The fuel cell runs at its steady, efficient best, quietly charging a built-in battery — the buffer — like a thermostat that tops up only when needed.

The buffer powers your devices and absorbs every spike, so the fuel cell never has to chase sudden demand.

When a big load hits, the buffer delivers up to 6 kW on demand — then the fuel cell quietly recharges it.

STEP 1 — the fuel cell charging the
buffer at a steady, efficient rate
STEP 2 — the buffer powering the load
and absorbing demand spikes
STEP 3 — a 6 kW surge delivered on demand,
then the cell recharging the buffer
What makes it different

Not a fuel cell. Not a battery. Both.

Stability

Steady 85%, always

The fuel cell stays at peak efficiency no matter how demand swings — instead of crashing under load.

Power-on-demand

Surges on tap

Delivers up to 6 kW for an hour to handle real-world spikes, then quietly recharges itself.

New category

A first of its kind

Combines a battery's instant power with a fuel cell's endless generation — a class of device that didn't exist before.

At a glance

The essentials.

What it is
Fuel cell + battery buffer + intelligent charge-transfer regulator (QXR).
Continuous power
Up to 1.7 kW steady per blade (≈41 kWh/day).
Power-on-demand
Up to 6 kW for ~1 hour — handles surges like AC startup.
Efficiency held
Fuel cell stays at ~85% under any load (vs dropping to ~32% on direct drive).
Behavior
Self-recharging — steady-state, power-on-demand, and restore modes.
Category
Not a fuel cell, not a battery — a new class.

Technology / CoolEnergy™ Energy Bank
The system · all three, productized

CoolEnergy™ Energy Bank.

The whole platform in one cabinet — making hydrogen from solar, storing it safely as a solid, and turning it back into clean power around the clock.

PRODUCT HERO ILLUSTRATION
isometric of the Energy Bank cabinet
(water-heater sized) — cutaway showing
PowerBlades, solid-state H₂ store,
and control electronics
Why it matters

Clean power is only useful if it's there 24/7.

Solar stops at night, and batteries run down in hours at a steep cost per kilowatt-hour. To run a building — or a data center — you need power that never quits, without a grid connection or a generator.

COMPARISON — one Energy Bank cabinet beside three Tesla Powerwalls: size, weight, cost, and continuous-power callouts (wide)
How it works

Make it, store it, use it — on a loop.

One simple cycle, running around the clock.

Solar panels and water make hydrogen on-site — your own fuel, produced from sunlight.

The hydrogen is stored safely as a solid at low pressure — no high-pressure tanks, no explosion risk, ready whenever you need it.

Whenever power is needed, the Energy Bank converts hydrogen back to clean electricity — 24/7, rain or shine.

STEP 1 — solar panels and water
producing hydrogen on-site
STEP 2 — hydrogen stored as a solid at
low pressure (no explosion risk)
STEP 3 — hydrogen converted back to
clean electricity, 24/7
What makes it different

One unit does the work of many.

Economics

A fraction of the cost

One Energy Bank can replace three-plus Tesla Powerwalls — and after solar payback, the recurring energy cost approaches $0/kWh.

Endurance

Days, not hours

Unlike a battery that empties, it runs continuously — one hydrogen canister powers a home or office (HVAC included) for a full day. Add more for more days.

Scale

Building to data center

The same architecture scales from a wall-mounted cabinet to microgrid racks (120 kW) and multi-megawatt PowerStacks for AI data centers.
